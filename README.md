# Biochemical Fluoride Removal Using Reversible Fluidized Bed

## Overview

This project focuses on the removal of fluoride from contaminated water using a biochemical treatment process integrated with a reversible fluidized bed reactor. The study investigates the efficiency of fluoride adsorption, reactor performance, and operational parameters affecting the treatment process.

The system utilizes fluidization principles to enhance mass transfer, improve contact between adsorbent and water, and achieve effective fluoride removal.

---

## Objectives

* Remove excess fluoride from aqueous solutions.
* Analyze the performance of a reversible fluidized bed reactor.
* Study adsorption kinetics and equilibrium behavior.
* Evaluate the effect of operating parameters on fluoride removal efficiency.
* Develop a sustainable and cost-effective water treatment approach.

---

## Features

* Fluoride concentration analysis
* Reversible fluidized bed simulation
* Adsorption efficiency calculations
* Experimental data processing
* Graphical visualization of results
* Performance comparison under different operating conditions

---

## Methodology

1. Preparation of fluoride-contaminated water samples.
2. Selection and characterization of adsorbent material.
3. Operation of the reversible fluidized bed reactor.
4. Measurement of influent and effluent fluoride concentrations.
5. Analysis of adsorption efficiency.
6. Evaluation of reactor performance.

## Installation

```bash

```

Navigate to the project directory:

